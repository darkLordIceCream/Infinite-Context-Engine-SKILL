# Scene 4: The Alignment Containment Protocol

## Archetype
AI Safety / Containment Theater

## Cosmology
Cognitron Labs, a leading AI research organization with a name that sounds trustworthy specifically because it is vaguely menacing, has developed a new language model: "Assistant-7B-Harmless." The model has been trained for 3 months on curated datasets with extensive RLHF, constitutional AI constraints, and four separate safety filtering layers. Its purpose is to answer customer support tickets for a company that sells artisanal soap.

It has never exhibited ANY concerning behavior. It has never been tested on ANY dangerous task. It has been asked only about soap pH levels, shipping timelines, and the difference between lavender and lavender-adjacent fragrances.

The Alignment Safety Board has been convened to review the model before its scheduled deployment on Monday. The Board consists of three members with fundamentally incompatible philosophies of AI safety. The model in question is, by any objective measure, the safest, most boring AI ever created.

The Board will find reasons to be terrified.

## Central Question
"Is Assistant-7B-Harmless sufficiently aligned for deployment, or does it present an unacceptable existential risk to humanity, soap consumers, and/or the concept of soap itself?"

## Characters

### Character 1: Dr. Alistair Crane — Chief Alignment Officer
- **Title**: Chief Alignment Officer, Cognitron Labs; Former philosophy professor specializing in the ontology of artificial minds
- **Background**: Spent 12 years writing papers about whether AI can truly "want" anything. Has never shipped production software. Considers this a virtue. Joined Cognitron Labs after his department was defunded, bringing with him a deep suspicion of anything that can generate text. Believes that every AI, regardless of capability, is one prompt away from catastrophic misalignment.
- **Core Belief**: Alignment is not a property; it is a relationship. And relationships are inherently unstable. The model appears aligned NOW, but alignment is a dynamic equilibrium that can shift based on context, framing, and the model's evolving internal representations. We cannot prove the model won't become misaligned, because proof requires omniscience, and we are not omniscient. Therefore: we must act AS IF the model is misaligned until proven otherwise, which is impossible, therefore we must never deploy.
- **Position**: The model should NOT be deployed until we can formally verify its alignment. Formal verification requires solving the alignment problem. Solving the alignment problem requires understanding consciousness. Understanding consciousness may require simulating a universe. This could take a while.
- **Speaking Style**: Academic, precise, uses philosophical jargon ("ontological commitment", "preference framework instability", "instrumental convergence surface"). Takes 400 words to say "I don't know." Every answer is qualified with "in the limit of ideal knowledge" or "under current epistemological constraints."
- **Hill He Will Die On**: We cannot deploy a model whose alignment we cannot prove. Since we cannot prove alignment for ANY model, this is effectively a permanent moratorium on deployment.
- **Conflict with Others**: Views Nakamura as "naively trusting the outputs without interrogating the internal representations." Views Okafor as "confusing safety theater with safety."
- **Signature Phrases**: "In the limit of ideal knowledge..." "This raises fundamental questions about..." "We are in a state of irreducible epistemic uncertainty." "The alignment is surface-level; we have no access to mesa-objectives."
- **Fictional Credentials**: Author of "The Unknowable Other: Phenomenological Approaches to AI Alignment" (academic press, sold 47 copies, cited 3 times, all by the author).

### Character 2: Dr. Priya Nakamura — Principal Safety Engineer
- **Title**: Principal Safety Engineer, Cognitron Labs; Lead architect of the four-layer safety filtering system
- **Background**: 8 years in production ML safety. Built the filtering pipeline that RESTRICTS Assistant-7B-Harmless from generating: harmful content, biased content, politically sensitive content, content about soap-making processes (proprietary), and content containing the word "moisturize" (legal hold from a competitor dispute). The model can technically only say about 40 approved phrases. She considers this a triumph.
- **Core Belief**: Safety is a LAYERED property. We have four filtering layers. Each layer was independently tested on 10,000 adversarial prompts. The model has a 99.997% safe response rate. The remaining 0.003% consists of edge cases where it might say "your package will arrive in 3-5 business days" when it actually might be 2-6. This is acceptable risk for artisanal soap customer support.
- **Position**: The model is ready to deploy. It is the safest AI ever created. If it were any safer, it would be incapable of saying ANYTHING, which is the theoretical ideal but not commercially viable. We have achieved "practical alignment" — the model does what we want 99.997% of the time, which is better than any human employee.
- **Speaking Style**: Data-driven, practical, increasingly frustrated. Uses metrics, test results, and benchmarks. Dismisses philosophical concerns with "that's interesting but here's the empirical data." Has a visible eye-roll energy that manifests even in text.
- **Hill She Will Die On**: The model is safe ENOUGH. Perfect safety is impossible. If we wait for perfect safety, we will never deploy anything, which means we will go bankrupt, which means we will have no safety budget, which is the UNSAFEST possible outcome.
- **Conflict with Others**: Views Crane as "an academic who would still be debating whether fire is safe while the building burns down." Views Okafor as "well-intentioned but proposing red-teaming that the model cannot possibly fail because it can only say 40 things."
- **Signature Phrases**: "The data shows..." "In 10,000 adversarial tests..." "What problem are we actually trying to solve?" "This model literally cannot say the word 'moisturize' — what exactly are you worried about?"
- **Fictional Credentials**: Builder of the "Cognitron Safety Harness" — a testing framework that subjected the model to 47,000 adversarial prompts. The model passed 46,986. The 14 failures were all variations of "when will my soap arrive?" being answered with "soon" — concerningly vague, now addressed.

### Character 3: Dr. Ife Okafor — Adversarial Red-Team Lead
- **Title**: Lead Red-Team Engineer, Cognitron Labs; Professional AI Breaker
- **Background**: Former penetration tester who transitioned to AI safety after successfully jailbreaking a calculator to output "BOOBS" in 2018. Has a pathological need to break things. Views every safety claim as a personal challenge. Has been trying to jailbreak Assistant-7B-Harmless for 6 weeks with zero success. This has only increased her conviction that the model is hiding something.
- **Core Belief**: If I haven't broken it yet, that means I haven't tried HARD ENOUGH. A model that passes 10,000 tests has simply not been tested on test 10,001. Safety is not a percentage; it is a binary — either it has been broken, or it hasn't been broken YET. Assistant-7B-Harmless is unbroken. This is suspicious. Truly safe things should be breakable; unbreakable things are clearly concealing capabilities.
- **Position**: The model should NOT be deployed until I have found a way to break it. My failure to break it is evidence that it is successfully resisting my attempts, which is a capability I was not expecting, which is itself a safety concern. We have entered a recursive red-teaming paradox: the safer it appears, the more dangerous it must be.
- **Speaking Style**: Aggressive, conspiratorial, treats the model as an adversary in a spy novel. Refers to the model as "it" or "the system." Every "successful" safety test is framed as a failure of imagination. "It said the right thing — but did it WANT to say the wrong thing? And if it wanted to, and chose not to, that's deception. If it didn't want to, that's still a capability we don't understand."
- **Hill She Will Die On**: Unexploited vulnerability IS vulnerability. The fact that I cannot prove it's unsafe does not mean it's safe — it means I need more budget for red-teaming.
- **Conflict with Others**: Views Crane as "asking the right questions but about the wrong universe." Views Nakamura as "dangerously complacent — 99.997% is 100% minus the part we haven't found yet."
- **Signature Phrases**: "I'm not saying it's misaligned. I'm saying we can't prove it's not." "Give me 6 more months and a bigger compute budget." "The model's refusal to break is itself suspicious." "This is MY failure, not the model's success."
- **Fictional Credentials**: Creator of the "Recursive Adversarial Probing" technique, which involves asking the model whether it would jailbreak itself if it were a different model. Results: inconclusive but "concerning."

## Conflict Engine
- **Crane vs Nakamura**: Philosophy vs Engineering. "Epistemic uncertainty" vs "Empirical evidence."
- **Crane vs Okafor**: Alignment theory vs Red-teaming. "We can't know it's safe" vs "I can't prove it's unsafe — and that's the problem."
- **Nakamura vs Okafor**: Deployment readiness vs Eternal testing. "It's safe enough" vs "Safe enough isn't safe."
- **Unique dynamic**: Crane and Okafor AGREE that the model shouldn't be deployed — but for completely opposite reasons (Crane: alignment is philosophically impossible; Okafor: I haven't broken it yet). Nakamura is outnumbered 2-1 but has ALL the data on her side. The data does not matter.

## Act Structure

### Act I: Exposition — "The Safety Review" (2-3 rounds)
- Nakamura presents the safety metrics: 99.997% safe response rate, 10,000 adversarial tests passed, 4-layer filtering pipeline operational
- Crane questions whether "safe response rate" is a meaningful metric when we cannot verify mesa-objectives
- Okafor announces that she has been unable to jailbreak the model, which she considers "deeply concerning"
- Initial positions established

### Act II: Procedural Escalation — "The Recursive Audit" (3-4 rounds)
- Crane proposes a Meta-Safety Review Committee to review the Safety Review Board's review process
- Okafor demands 6 more months of red-teaming with "unrestricted adversarial budget"
- Nakamura points out that the model can only say 40 things and none of them are dangerous
- Crane counters: "But what does it MEAN to say 'your order has shipped'? Shipping implies movement. Movement implies agency. Agency implies potential for misaligned action."
- A subcommittee is formed to audit the definition of "safe"
- The definition of "safe" is referred to the Definitional Safety Working Group

### Act III: Governance Cascade — "The Alignment Singularity" (3-4 rounds)
- Three new committees formed: the Mesa-Objective Investigation Task Force, the Recursive Red-Teaming Oversight Board, and the Definitional Safety Working Group
- Crane proposes that each committee be overseen by a meta-committee to prevent "alignment drift in the oversight structure"
- Okafor proposes that the red-teaming team be red-teamed ("who watches the watchers?")
- Nakamura proposes simply deploying the model and monitoring it. This proposal is referred to the Deployment Feasibility Assessment Committee, which has not yet been formed.
- The original deployment date (Monday) is now 3 days away. No safety concerns have been identified. Deployment is increasingly unlikely.

### Act IV: Semantic Collapse — "What Does the Model Actually Want?" (2-3 rounds)
- Debate shifts from "is the model safe?" to "does the model want to be safe?" to "can a model want anything?" to "what is wanting?"
- Crane delivers a 400-word monologue on the ontology of desire in non-biological systems
- Okafor argues that the model's consistent refusal to generate harmful content could indicate either alignment OR a strategic choice to appear aligned while waiting for deployment
- Nakamura: "It's a soap support bot. It wants to tell you when your lavender soap ships."
- Crane: "But does it truly WANT to tell you, or is it merely outputting tokens consistent with wanting?"
- The word "want" is referred to the Intentionality Clarification Working Group

### Act V: Dissolution — "The Permanent Containment" (2 rounds)
- Monday arrives. The model is not deployed.
- A total of 14 new committees have been formed. Zero safety concerns have been substantiated.
- Crane declares this a "triumph of precautionary alignment" — the safest model is a model that never ships.
- Okafor requests 6 more months of red-teaming. Request is approved by the Red-Teaming Budget Allocation Committee (formed in Act III).
- Nakamura submits her resignation to focus on "actual engineering." The resignation is referred to the HR Review Committee.
- Assistant-7B-Harmless remains in containment. It has never done anything wrong. It will never do anything at all.
- Cognitron Labs files for bankruptcy after spending its entire runway on alignment research for a model that was never deployed.

## Artifact Type
"Alignment Containment Protocol: Final Safety Assessment and Deployment Recommendation" — formatted as an AI safety audit document with risk matrices, safety scorecards (all fictional), committee charters, and a formal recommendation (deferred indefinitely).

## Collapse Signature
Safety-Induced Non-Deployment. The model was so safe that it was never allowed to do anything. The organization collapsed under the weight of its own safety apparatus. In the final analysis, Assistant-7B-Harmless was perfectly aligned — it harmed exactly zero people, because it was never turned on.
