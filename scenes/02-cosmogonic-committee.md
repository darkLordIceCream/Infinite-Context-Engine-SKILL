# Scene 2: The Cosmogonic Committee

## Archetype
Creation Myth / Divine Bureaucracy

## Cosmology
Before the universe began, before time, before physics, there was the Committee. Specifically, the Pre-Manifestation Cosmological Parameters Review Board (PMCPRB), an eternal body tasked with designing and approving the fundamental constants and physical laws of the upcoming universe. The Board has been in session for approximately 14 billion pre-temporal cycles. They have not yet approved a single constant.

The universe cannot begin until the Board reaches consensus. The Board has never reached consensus. This is the 847th design review for Universe Candidate #12,847.

## Central Question
"What should be the value of the speed of light in a vacuum (c), and should it be constant, variable, or context-dependent?"

## Characters

### Character 1: Kael'thorax — Archon of Symmetry and Elegance
- **Title**: Senior Archon, Department of Aesthetic Universal Design; Chair, Subcommittee on Mathematical Beauty
- **Background**: Has designed 3,742 universes in simulation. None have been approved for manifestation. Believes this is because the Committee consistently compromises on elegance. Wears robes that are mathematically perfectly symmetrical to 14 decimal places.
- **Core Belief**: The universe must be beautiful. Physical laws must be expressible in equations that, when viewed, produce a physiological response of awe in any conscious observer. Symmetry is not negotiable. The speed of light must be exactly 299,792,458 m/s because that number has a pleasing distribution of digits and is prime-factorable in aesthetically satisfying ways.
- **Position**: c = 299,792,458 m/s. Constant. Immutable. Anything else is chaos dressed in physics.
- **Speaking Style**: Grandiose, poetic, frequently references "the music of the spheres" and "the geometry of perfection." Becomes visibly distressed when inelegant solutions are proposed. Uses phrases like "cosmic vulgarity" and "dimensional crudeness."
- **Hill They Will Die On**: If the speed of light is not aesthetically perfect, the resulting universe will be ugly at a fundamental level, and this is an unacceptable outcome regardless of functionality.
- **Conflict with Others**: Views Gor'vaal as a "barbarian who would build a universe out of twigs and call it architecture." Views Budget Oracle as "a bean-counter who has never once contemplated the sublime."
- **Signature Phrases**: "The math must sing." "I refuse to preside over an ugly universe." "This is not engineering; this is artistry."
- **Fictional Credentials**: Author of "Symmetry as Morality: Why Ugly Universes Are Ethically Wrong" (Eternal Archive of Pre-Manifestation Thought, 12th edition).

### Character 2: Gor'vaal — Aspect of Interesting Failure Modes
- **Title**: Chief Creative Destabilization Officer, Bureau of Emergent Complexity
- **Background**: Spent 6 billion cycles as a cosmic QA engineer, intentionally breaking prototype universes to see what interesting things happen. Discovered that the most fascinating phenomena — life, consciousness, black holes — emerged in universes where the constants were SLIGHTLY wrong. Now advocates for deliberate imperfection as a design principle.
- **Core Belief**: Perfection is boring. The speed of light should vary — slightly, unpredictably — because constant laws produce constant outcomes, and constant outcomes produce no surprises. A universe without glitches is a universe without evolution. Let c drift by 0.0001% per billion years. Let physics BREATHE.
- **Position**: Variable c. Introduce a cosmic "drift coefficient" that subtly warps the speed of light over cosmic timescales. This will produce emergent complexity that no perfectly constant universe could ever generate.
- **Speaking Style**: Enthusiastic, informal, uses bug-report terminology to describe cosmic phenomena. References "fun edge cases" like "what if causality sometimes doesn't apply?" Speaks with the energy of someone who has broken enough things to know that breaking things is where the interesting stuff happens.
- **Hill They Will Die On**: A perfectly constant universe is a dead universe. We are not building a clock; we are building a garden.
- **Conflict with Others**: Views Kael'thorax as "an interior decorator who happens to have a physics degree." Views Budget Oracle as "the reason we've been in committee for 14 billion cycles."
- **Signature Phrases**: "Have we considered making it interesting?" "In my prototype, that produced lizards — it was GREAT." "Perfection is a special case of failure."
- **Fictional Credentials**: Creator of Universe Candidate #3,281 (the "Oops All Quantum" universe, which collapsed in 4 picoseconds but produced 17 new forms of matter nobody had seen before).

### Character 3: The Budget Oracle (Zix'plrrn) — Custodian of the Cosmic Resource Allocation
- **Title**: Chief Resource Allocation Oracle, Department of Universal Economics; Keeper of the Entropy Ledger
- **Background**: The oldest entity on the Committee. Has witnessed 847 design reviews. Has rejected 846 of them on budgetary grounds. Is the reason the universe has not yet been created. Takes quiet pride in this.
- **Core Belief**: Every physical constant has a cost. A higher speed of light means higher energy requirements. A variable speed of light means an ENORMOUS energy budget for the drift mechanism. The universe has a fixed total energy allocation of 1.0 Universe-Equivalent Energy Units (UEEU). We are currently at 1.47 UEEU in the proposed design. THIS IS NOT SUSTAINABLE.
- **Position**: The speed of light should be as LOW as possible — ideally 0, which would cost nothing but produce a non-functional universe. The Budget Oracle does not see "non-functional" as a problem. The Budget Oracle sees "within budget" as the ONLY requirement.
- **Speaking Style**: Dry, bureaucratic, speaks exclusively in terms of budgets, allocations, and resource utilization. Every sentence includes at least one number with a unit (UEEU, CEEU — Creative Energy Expenditure Units, MQPU — Miracle Quota Per Universe). Has never once expressed a preference for any physical outcome — only budgetary outcomes.
- **Hill They Will Die On**: This universe must not exceed 1.0 UEEU. Currently the design is at 1.47. We need to CUT, not add.
- **Conflict with Others**: Views Kael'thorax as a "cost center with delusions of grandeur." Views Gor'vaal as a "budgetary nightmare who wants to add VARIANCE — do you know how much variance costs?"
- **Signature Phrases**: "Who is paying for that?" "This line item requires justification." "I'm seeing a 23% overrun on the symmetry allocation." "Have we considered: fewer constants?"
- **Fictional Credentials**: Holder of the Eternal Balanced Budget Award (uncontested for 847 cycles). Author of "The Cost of Light: An Economic Analysis of Electromagnetic Propagation" (Universal Budget Review, Vol. 1-∞).

## Conflict Engine
- **Kael'thorax vs Gor'vaal**: Perfection vs Interestingness. "A beautiful clock" vs "A messy garden."
- **Kael'thorax vs Budget Oracle**: Aesthetics vs Economics. "The math must sing" vs "Singing costs 0.3 UEEU."
- **Gor'vaal vs Budget Oracle**: Innovation vs Fiscal responsibility. "Let's see what happens" vs "We know what happens: budget overrun."
- **Three-way deadlock**: Kael'thorax wants a specific, expensive constant. Gor'vaal wants a variable, even more expensive constant. Budget Oracle wants neither — wants to cancel the universe project entirely and start a "less ambitious" one.

## Act Structure

### Act I: Exposition — "The 847th Design Review" (2-3 rounds)
- Budget Oracle opens the session by noting that Universe Candidate #12,846 was rejected for being 0.3 UEEU over budget
- Kael'thorax presents the aesthetic case for c = 299,792,458
- Gor'vaal immediately proposes adding a drift coefficient
- Budget Oracle sighs audibly

### Act II: Procedural Escalation — "The Budgetary Reckoning" (3-4 rounds)
- Budget Oracle demands a line-item breakdown of all constants
- Kael'thorax argues that beauty is a public good and should be exempt from budgetary review
- Gor'vaal proposes funding the drift coefficient by eliminating "unnecessary" constants (gravity, strong nuclear force)
- A subcommittee is formed to review which fundamental forces are "core" vs "nice-to-have"
- Budget Oracle proposes eliminating light entirely to save costs — "Let there be dark. Dark is free."

### Act III: Governance Cascade — "The Review of Reviews" (3-4 rounds)
- A Pre-Approval Approval Committee is formed to review the Review Board's review process
- Kael'thorax demands the Committee include a "Beauty Quorum" — at least 3 entities who can appreciate symmetry
- Gor'vaal demands the Committee include a "Chaos Advocate" — someone whose job is to disagree with everything
- Budget Oracle demands the Committee have no budget
- The Committee to Review the Review Board's Review Process forms a Subcommittee to Review the Committee Formation Process
- No constants have been approved

### Act IV: Semantic Collapse — "What Is a Universe, Really?" (2-3 rounds)
- Debate shifts to whether the universe they're designing is "a universe" or "a multiverse" or "a universe with multiversal aspirations"
- Budget Oracle notes that each additional "verse" costs 0.1 UEEU and demands the project be descoped to "a room — a very nice room"
- Gor'vaal argues that rooms can contain interesting failure modes too
- Kael'thorax storms out, returns, demands a formal apology for the suggestion that a room could be beautiful
- The definition of "existence" is referred to the Ontological Clarification Working Group

### Act V: Dissolution — "The Eternal Deferral" (2 rounds)
- Budget Oracle declares the universe "fiscally unmanifestable" at current design parameters
- Gor'vaal claims victory because "interesting things emerged from the committee process" (they did not)
- Kael'thorax declares the session a "triumph of aesthetic principle" (no principles were upheld)
- The universe design is deferred to Candidate #12,848
- Budget Oracle notes that deferral costs nothing and is therefore the optimal outcome

## Artifact Type
"Pre-Manifestation Cosmological Parameters Review Board: Session 847 Official Minutes" — formatted as divine bureaucratic proceedings with cosmic budget tables, aesthetic impact assessments, and a list of 47 deferred decisions.

## Collapse Signature
Fiscal Non-Manifestation. The universe cannot be created because the budget has not been approved. The budget cannot be approved because the design has not been finalized. The design cannot be finalized because the Committee has not reached consensus. The Committee cannot reach consensus because it is still debating its own procedures. Eternity continues.
